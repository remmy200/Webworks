Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol

Definition:

This is the fundamental communication language used by the internet. Internet Protocol(IP) is responsible for addressing and routing of packets while the Transmission Control Protocol(TCP) ensures the data is delivered reliably and in the correct order. Together, TCP/IP enables devices to communicate and exchange data across networks, forming the backbone of the internet.

Simple Explanation

Think of TCP/IP like a delivery system. When you send data over the internet, TCP breaks the data into small pieces called packets and ensures they are delivered correctly. IP is responsible for addressing and sending those packets to the correct destination.

Key Components

  • TCP (Transmission Control Protocol): Breaks data into packets and ensures they arrive correctly and in order
  • IP (Internet Protocol): Handles addressing and directs packets to the right destination

Key Points

  • It is the foundation of the internet
  • Ensures reliable communication between devices
  • Breaks large data into smaller packets
  • Works together to send and receive data efficiently

Example

When you send a message or open a website, TCP/IP ensures the data travels across the internet and reaches the correct device without errors.
